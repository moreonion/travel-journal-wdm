The UK government is helping big business take over Africa’s food.

Big companies like Monsanto and Coca Cola are working with governments to make it easier for them to get their hands on land in Africa, and push small farmers to buy new seeds from them every year along with dangerous chemical pesticides year. But if small farmers in Africa lose their land and control of their seeds, they won’t be able to make a decent living and grow food for themselves and their communities.

At the moment, farmers are able to save seeds to use the following year, or swap seeds with other farmers. But if they’re not able to do this, they will have to spend more money on seeds that can only be used once, as well as chemical pesticides needed to grow crops from those seeds. That will cost them a lot of money, pushing more people into poverty.

Shockingly, UK aid money is helping to make this happen through the New Alliance for Food Security and Nutrition.

Later this month Justine Greening, Secretary of State for International Development, will represent the UK at a New Alliance meeting in New York.

WDM is calling on the UK government to withdraw from the New Alliance, and support small farmers instead.
